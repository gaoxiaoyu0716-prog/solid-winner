01 — Context & Goals

The Mission: Leading Design for a National-Level Event

The Sole Architect

As the only interaction designer in the department, I took full ownership of Weibo's most critical annual growth campaign — a national-level event reaching 100M+ daily active users.

The Problem

In an oversaturated competitive landscape, users had developed "red envelope fatigue" toward homogeneous campaign mechanics. The challenge was to reignite engagement through interaction innovation.

02 — Discovery & Research

Strategic Benchmarking: Beyond the "MUSE" Standards

Benchmarking

Conducted a deep competitive analysis against MUSE Design Awards winning cases — particularly Taobao's Spring Festival campaign — to identify the ceiling of interaction quality in large-scale event design.

The Pivot

Shifted the core strategy from a "transaction-driven" model to a "socially-driven" one — transforming collection tasks into social propagation mechanics that leveraged peer networks for organic growth.

Competitive Analysis Matrix

03 — Architecture & Adaptive Design

Dynamic Architecture: Context-Aware Layout Strategy

Designed a Hub-and-Spoke model where the main venue radiates to sub-venues, creating a clear navigational hierarchy for complex campaign content.

💡 Core Innovation — Dynamic Layout Optimization

Phase 1 — The Collector: Before completion, the card-collection module occupies 60% of the first screen, maximising core task exposure.

Phase 2 — The Challenger: After completion, the card area auto-collapses, surfacing the Horse Card and Bump Card modules. This eliminates redundant scrolling and boosts high-frequency feature CTR.

Resilience

Designed a multi-tier graceful degradation strategy with skeleton screens to maintain experience quality under extreme concurrency loads.

04 — Core Interaction & Mechanism

The Gameplay: A Multi-Layered Incentive Ecosystem

💡 Asset Circulation

Proposed a "2-for-1" exchange mechanism — trading 2 Fortune Horse Cards for 1 Spirit Horse Card. This created an "exit for surplus assets," connecting the entire gameplay chain and solving asset inflation.

💡 Emotional Metaphor — Bump Card

Introduced a "Male Horse + Female Horse" social pairing concept, transforming mechanical matching into a ritualised social interaction filled with ceremonial delight.

💡 Gamification Hierarchy

Proposed a tiered Spirit Horse Card system — Normal → Advanced → Challenge — using difficulty curve management to sustain 22 consecutive days of engagement.

The Starlight Bridge

Celebrity voice cards bridged main and sub-venues, leveraging fandom loyalty as a traffic conduit between campaign modules.

05 — Interaction Craft

Transition Logic: Elevating the Reveal Experience

Motion Principles

Applied Anticipation and Exaggeration principles from animation theory. Maximised the "wow factor" of the reveal moment without disrupting the operational flow.

Haptic Feedback

Designed tactile vibration patterns synchronised with motion design, reinforcing the physical sensation of "catching a horse" — turning a digital tap into a visceral experience.

08 — Collaboration & Leadership

Advocating for the User: The Pop-up Debate

Situation

The operations team proposed a full-screen pop-up modal to push campaign tasks — a high-visibility but highly disruptive approach.

My Action

I held firm on the user experience baseline. Presented data on pop-up dismissal rates and advocated for a non-intrusive notification system that respected user autonomy while still achieving business KPIs.

Result

The non-intrusive approach was shipped, maintaining user trust while meeting engagement targets — proving that UX advocacy and business goals are not mutually exclusive.

09 — Data-Driven Iteration

Tactical Pivot: Combating Mid-Campaign Fatigue

On Day 10, real-time data revealed a significant engagement drop — the mid-campaign fatigue point. Rather than accepting the decline, I proposed and rapidly shipped an emergency intervention.

The Fix — 12 PM Daily Flash-Drop

Introduced a daily limited-time word-guessing challenge at noon, leveraging the Scarcity Effect to create urgency. The flash event successfully reversed the activity curve and re-established daily engagement rhythm.
